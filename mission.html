In August of 2023, I ended my work for the Federal COVID-19 response and left government to start my PhD in Biostatistics at the University of Minnesota. For a few months, I was a normal PhD student but then on November 14, 2023, I met a young, seemingly successful tech CEO on an online dating app. He was hungry, I was hungry, so we did something that seems somewhat rare for young gay men in this day and age -- we went on an actual date and it was magical. He was a great conversationalist, could keep up with me intellectually, and he seemed \emph{very interested} in what I had to say. There were \textcolor{antagonist}{red flags} from the start, which I wrote off as eccentricity at first, but he was hiding something \emph{much} darker. I broke up with him in March of 2024, right before a trip to NYC where I was going to introduce him to my parents. During this trip, he went missing and resurfaced a week later. He claims he was in a coma and once we reconnected in Minneapolis, he explained to me \emph{in my own words} how he had been a terrible partner and why the relationship didn't work out the first time around. For once, he seemed to understand my concerns and told me what I needed to hear to give him another chance. Once I moved in with him in 04/2024, my personal hell began. While living with him in the \$8K/month penthouse he was renting, I witnessed extremely erratic and terrifying behavior, ranging from idealization (i.e., \emph{``you're the one''}, \emph{``you're perfect''}, buying me ridiculously lavish presents, etc) to devaluation (e.g., \emph{``I'm a billionaire and you're just a childish PhD student''}, \emph{``if I die, the entire world will feel my absence''}). I tried to leave him but he was threatening me with frivolous lawsuits and smear campaigns, among other very effective tactics of coercive control. I managed to escape by documenting my entire life. Even though I'm not a psychologist, I develop novel statistical methods for analyzing brain imaging data, so I have a very good understanding of mental illness. So I did what every reasonable scientist would do and I went down the list of diagnostic categories in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-V). I pieced it together that he has undiagnosed borderline PD, since he met 9 out of 9 criteria. What I missed was the narcissistic PD and antisocial PD (i.e., sociopathy). You see, the DSM is a broken diagnostic system that seems to be right only twice a month, in WL's case \footnote{Since then, I learned about the Hierarchical Taxonomy of Psychopathology (HiTOP) model from one of the most brilliant clinical psychologists of our time and one of the nicest human beings I've ever met.}. After reading up on those conditions, I realized that I was in danger and that there would be repercussions for breaking up with him. I started planning my exit strategy with the help of other people whose lives he's ruined and collecting evidence that documents the domestic abuse and fraud. In 07/2024, I briefed my PhD program about the situation and they excused me from the written preliminary exam I was scheduled to take that summer (the first major milestone in the PhD program). Two days later, he was behaving very erratically so I packed my belongings in front of a witness while I ended the relationship. When he got really angry, he tried to strangle me while my witness (his investor Stefan Nitchoff) held him back, as I fled with my canary and some essentials.
As you can imagine, institutions have failed miserably at protecting me, and in many ways have only made things worse. The criminal justice system in Minneapolis is very lax when it comes to prosecuting domestic violence, so I expect that he'll end up with a slap on the wrist, but the biggest betrayal is by my PhD program. In a misguided attempt to be equitable and trauma-informed, their mismanagement of my situation created a situation where my funding will run out before I'm finished with my dissertation. They acknowledge their mistake but have done \emph{nothing} to rectify it and they're not going to. In fact, these days they seem to be blaming me for \emph{their} poor decision making. They have created a hostile and unsafe work environment. Once I noticed my adviser using some of the same manipulation tactics that WL would use, I fired him from his role as my dissertation adviser and confronted the Division Chair about the hypocrisy of bragging about health equity when their practices have actively harmed a domestic violence survivor in their own backyard. I filed a grievance through the Graduate Labor Union, operationalized Title IX, and started escalating further after all of these internal processes were inadequate at protecting me from workplace bullying, institutional gaslighting, and otherwise toxic behavior. I'm currently being evaluated for Complex-PTSD as a result of these events. I'm left all alone to figure out a solution while academia reenacts a double-abuse that is sadly too common for survivors.
